Guidance & Support

Legal Help

Workplace harassment is illegal under various federal and state laws. You have the right to work in an environment free from harassment and discrimination.

  • Title VII of the Civil Rights Act
  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
  • Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA)
  • Equal Pay Act

Legal resources:

  • Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC)
  • Workplace Fairness
  • American Bar Association - Workplace Harassment Resources

Mental Health Support

Experiencing harassment can be emotionally draining. Here are some strategies that may help:

  • Practice self-care and mindfulness
  • Maintain a support network of trusted friends and family
  • Set boundaries to protect your wellbeing
  • Keep a journal to process your experiences
  • Seek professional help when needed

Mental health resources:

  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI)
  • Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA)
  • Talkspace Online Therapy
Crisis Support: National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-8255
Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741

Whistleblower Protection

Whistleblower laws protect employees who report illegal activities or dangers to public health and safety. These protections are designed to shield you from retaliation.

  • Whistleblower Protection Act
  • Sarbanes-Oxley Act (for corporate whistleblowers)
  • False Claims Act
  • Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA)

What constitutes retaliation?

  • Termination or demotion
  • Reduction in hours or pay
  • Reassignment to less desirable duties
  • Hostile work environment
  • Threats or intimidation
  • Negative performance reviews that are unwarranted

Whistleblower resources:

  • OSHA Whistleblower Protection Program
  • SEC Whistleblower Program
  • National Whistleblower Center

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my identity really anonymous?

Your information is encrypted and stored without any identifiers.

What happens after I submit a report?

The report is reviewed by an investigator and you can track the status with the case ID.

How do companies take action on reports?

They follow internal procedures and may launch investigations based on severity.
